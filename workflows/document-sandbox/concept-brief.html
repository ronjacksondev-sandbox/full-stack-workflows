Duo Budget

Business Concept & Validation Brief

Overview

Duo Budget is a budgeting solution designed to help people understand how their time and money work together. By applying shared categories to both resources, Duo Budget provides a unified view of personal resource allocation.

1. Problem Statement

People struggle to understand the relationship between their time and their money because existing tools treat these resources separately. Budgeting apps focus on dollars, while productivity tools focus on hours — leaving users without a unified view of how their choices in one domain affect the other.

2. Target Audience

Primary Audience

Busy, average‑income individuals — often with one or more dependents — who need a simple, intuitive way to manage limited time and limited money without juggling multiple apps.

Secondary Audience

People who are curious about their personal resource usage and want deeper insight into how their time and money interact.

3. Value Proposition

For busy people, Duo Budget is a budgeting solution that unifies the management of time and money by applying shared categories to both resources. This helps users understand how their choices in one area impact the other, creating a more holistic picture of personal resource allocation.

4. Core Features

  • Shared categories for time and money: categorize both hours and dollars using the same set of categories.
  • Yearly budget goal projection: plan both time and money across the year, aligned with personal goals.
  • Time and money allocation dashboard: visualize how hours and dollars are spent, revealing patterns and tradeoffs.

5. Business Model

Duo Budget will begin with a lean approach, with the recommended model being a freemium structure:

  • Free tier: basic tracking and shared categories
  • Premium tier: projections, advanced analytics, and customization

This model supports incremental development and allows the product to grow alongside user needs.

6. Validation Summary

Duo Budget will be developed using a lean, agile approach. Early versions of the product will serve as the primary source of validation. A small group of pre‑selected beta users will test the initial iterations, providing real usage data and feedback. This data will guide decisions about whether to continue, pivot, or pause development.

7. Success Criteria

Duo Budget will be considered validated if:

  • Beta users find the app functional and intuitive
  • Users return consistently over several weeks
  • Feedback confirms the value of the unified time‑and‑money model
  • Some users express willingness to use the app long‑term or pay for premium features
