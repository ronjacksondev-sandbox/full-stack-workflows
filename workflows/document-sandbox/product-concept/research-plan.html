Duo Budget – Pre-Charter Research Plan

Background

Duo Budget is exploring whether people see value in connecting their time and money through a shared category model. Before committing to a full MVP build, we need to understand how people currently manage these resources, what tools they use, what frustrates them, and whether a unified model resonates.

Research goals

  • Understand how people currently track time and money.
  • Identify pain points in existing tools and workflows.
  • Explore whether users see time and money as connected resources.
  • Validate interest in a unified time-and-money tool.
  • Assess willingness to pay for such a tool.
  • Gather language and mental models for future messaging.

Research questions

  • How do people currently track money?
  • How do people currently track time?
  • What is difficult or frustrating about each?
  • Do people ever compare the value of their time to the cost of outsourcing?
  • What motivates people to do things themselves versus paying for help?
  • Would a unified view of time and money be useful or confusing?
  • What features matter most in a tool like this?
  • What would users expect to pay, if anything?

Methodology

  • Survey (quantitative + qualitative): Gather broad patterns and validate assumptions.
  • Follow-up interviews: Explore mental models, emotions, and deeper reasoning.
  • Light competitive scan: Understand how users perceive alternatives (envelopes, spreadsheets, apps).

Participants

Inclusion criteria

  • Adults 18+.
  • People who track money, time, or both.
  • People who use at least one planning or budgeting tool.
  • Mix of household types (solo, couple, family).
  • Mix of financial confidence levels.

Exclusion criteria

  • People who do not track time or money in any way.
  • People unwilling to reflect on their habits.

Screening questions

  • Do you currently track your money in any way?
  • Do you currently track your time in any way?
  • How often do you use planning or tracking tools?
  • Are you willing to answer questions about your planning habits?
  • Are you at least 18 years old?

Survey instrument

  1. What is your age range?
  2. What best describes your household type?
  3. What tools do you currently use to track money?
  4. What tools do you currently use to track time?
  5. What is the hardest part about managing your time?
  6. What is the hardest part about managing your money?
  7. What are ways you try to save money by doing a project yourself?
  8. How much would you pay for a tool that helps you manage time and money together?

Success criteria

  • We identify clear, repeated pain points.
  • At least some users express interest in a unified time-and-money model.
  • We gather language that helps shape messaging.
  • We learn what features matter most for an MVP.
  • We understand willingness to pay (or lack thereof).

Timeline

  • Week 1: Finalize survey and screener.
  • Week 2: Collect survey responses.
  • Week 3: Conduct follow-up interviews.
  • Week 4: Synthesize findings and draft recommendations.

Deliverables

  • Survey results summary.
  • Interview insights.
  • Key themes and opportunity areas.
  • Recommendations for MVP scope.
  • Risks and assumptions.
  • Suggested next steps.
